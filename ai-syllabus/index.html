AI From First Principles A Reading & Replication Syllabus

Structured around the four learning objectives — discriminative, generative, representational, and value/policy — the architecture spine that feeds them, and the post-training layers stacked on top. Each unit pairs canonical papers with a project to replicate, climbing from a laptop-scale "hello world" to a recognizable approximation of the state of the art.

How to use this. Each ladder is independent — climb them in any order, though Unit 0 (architecture) and the discriminative ladder are the gentlest start. Each rung pairs a paper with a project.

Projects are tagged by the compute they realistically need:

LAPTOP laptop / Colab, minutes–hours GPU a single GPU READ read-to-understand, or replicate scaled-down

CAPSTONE

Build the Tree Yourself

architecture × method

Demonstrate "trained vs. develops"

GPU Pretrain a small (~10–50M param) decoder-only Transformer on a text corpus (TinyStories or a Wikipedia subset) with next-token prediction — the generative objective. Then, without any further training, extract its hidden states and use them as sentence embeddings for a retrieval task. Measure how good that "developed" representational capability is versus a purpose-built embedding model. This is the whole thesis in one experiment: a generative-objective model doing a representational job for free, because the architecture is general enough that the training objective isn't the only thing it can express.

APPENDIX

Practical Notes

logistics
  • Frameworks. PyTorch throughout, except Project 0 (by hand in NumPy). torchvision for MNIST/CIFAR; gymnasium for RL environments; Hugging Face transformers + datasets + trl for the LLM and post-training units.
  • Compute. Most green/amber projects fit a single consumer GPU or free Colab. The red papers are deliberately read-only or LoRA/scaled-down — don't train Stable Diffusion or a Nature-DQN Atari agent from scratch without the hardware and patience.
  • Order. For a single through-line rather than four parallel climbs: Unit 0 → Discriminative → Representational → Generative → Value/Policy → Post-Training → Capstone. This roughly follows increasing infrastructure overhead.
  • Datasets. MNIST and CIFAR-10 carry the first three ladders; text8/Wikipedia for representational; Gymnasium/MuJoCo/Atari for RL; instruction and preference sets (UltraFeedback, etc.) for post-training.

A map, not a contract — skip the red read-only units freely on a first pass, and return to them once the hands-on rungs have built your intuition.
